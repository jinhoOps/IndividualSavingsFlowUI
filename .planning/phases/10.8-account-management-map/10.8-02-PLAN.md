## Overview

Build the full Account Map experience on top of the route and draft foundation from Plan 01. The page opens with a readable account-flow map first, uses relationship colors and short labels, hides exact amounts in the overview, and reveals details only after selection.

Purpose: deliver the at-a-glance account-management map requested in the Phase 10.8 goal while keeping detail and editing relationship-first.

Output: an interactive Account Map page with map, compact summary, selection detail, candidate review, and page-owned save behavior.

## Scope

In scope:

- Render account nodes and relationship edges from Account Map draft data.
- Make relationship types visually distinct using colors and short labels per D-05.
- Keep exact amounts hidden in the overview per D-04.
- Reveal monthly amount, payment day, memo, confidence, and source provenance on selected account/relationship per D-15.
- Provide fixed-expense candidate review where users can accept or exclude candidates before they become relationships per D-12.
- Persist user edits and candidate decisions to Account Map-owned draft state per D-10.
- Keep desktop summary small and mobile summary compact so the map remains first-screen dominant per D-14, D-16, and D-17.

Out of scope:

- No relationship-type filter tabs.
- No ordinary budget/category editing.
- No write-back to Main.
- No live bank/card scraping.

## Risks/Notes

- Avoid map labels that expose exact amounts; use type labels/counts and detail reveal only.
- Candidate review must not become a budget screen; it is only for recurring account-management relationships.
- Store relationship `type` for future filters, but do not render filter tabs in this phase.
- The renderer should be route-local to avoid increasing Main complexity.

# Phase 10.6.1 Context: Legacy Editor Removal and Detail Modal Capability Absorption

## Source Inputs

- `CONTEXT.md`
- `docs/adr/0001-financial-detail-modal-is-the-only-primary-editor.md`
- Phase 10.6 completed plans and verification

## User-Facing Language

**Summary Surface**: The default Step 1 surface where users confirm financial information, scan totals, and open deeper flows. It is not the place for ordinary financial item editing.
Avoid: Main editor, default editor, dashboard editor.

**Financial Detail Modal**: The primary editing surface for Step 1 financial settings. Users should be able to complete income, expense, savings, investment, and account edits here without relying on another financial editor.
Avoid: Category modal, new modal, advanced editor.

**Auxiliary Advanced Editor**: A secondary or legacy financial editing surface that may remain for compatibility or narrow fallback cases. It should not be treated as the normal user path.
Avoid: Main editor, primary editor.

**Income Account Allocation**: The split of one income item across one or more destination accounts. The read row should show only the representative deposit account, while allocation editing belongs inside the income row edit state in the Financial Detail Modal.
Avoid: Manual transfer, separate transfer rule.

**Savings Maturity Month**: The month when a savings item stops receiving new contributions in the simulation. After this month, the savings item should no longer receive new monthly contributions, while the accumulated balance remains in projection calculations; investment items do not use maturity month.
Avoid: Display-only maturity, memo date.

**Item-Level Savings Yield**: The annual yield attached to one savings item instead of the global default yield. It belongs with maturity month inside a collapsed additional settings area of the savings row edit state.
Avoid: Global savings yield, display rate.

## ADR Constraint

Step 1 uses the summary surface for reading and scanning financial state, while ordinary financial edits must complete inside the Financial Detail Modal. Hidden legacy editor DOM, the item-editor controller, the global item-editor pending bar, and tests that require that secondary editor are not part of the target UX. Any remaining useful capability must be absorbed into the Financial Detail Modal instead of preserving a second editing surface.

## Decisions

- **D-01 Primary editor boundary:** Financial Detail Modal is the only primary ordinary Step 1 financial editing surface.
- **D-02 Summary surface boundary:** The Step 1 summary surface remains scan-and-open only; it should not host ordinary financial item editing controls.
- **D-03 Legacy editor removal:** Hidden legacy editor DOM, item-editor controller wiring, and global item-editor pending bar are removal targets unless a narrow compatibility fallback is proven necessary.
- **D-04 Capability absorption:** Useful legacy-only capabilities must move into Financial Detail Modal row edit states before their old surface is removed.
- **D-05 Income allocation location:** Income account allocation belongs inside the income row edit state; compact read rows show a representative deposit account only.
- **D-06 Savings maturity semantics:** Savings maturity month stops future monthly contributions after the maturity month while retaining accumulated balance in projections.
- **D-07 Item-level savings yield location:** Item-level savings yield belongs with maturity month inside collapsed savings row additional settings.
- **D-08 Language cleanup:** User-facing copy must avoid "main editor", "primary editor", "new modal", "advanced editor", "manual transfer", "separate transfer rule", "display-only maturity", and "global savings yield" when those terms conflict with this phase's model.
- **D-09 Regression boundary:** Sanitizer, persistence, Sankey rendering, share/import, and Phase 10.6 row-editing/pending-bar behavior must remain the durable compatibility boundary.

## Scope

### Must Include

- Remove normal user entry points into the legacy financial item editor.
- Remove or isolate hidden secondary editor DOM and global item-editor pending controls no longer used by the target UX.
- Absorb income account allocation into Financial Detail Modal income rows.
- Absorb savings maturity month and item-level savings yield into Financial Detail Modal savings rows.
- Update tests that currently require the legacy editor path so they assert Financial Detail Modal behavior instead.
- Preserve Step 1 persistence, sanitizer, Sankey, share/import, and Phase 10.6 modal behavior.

### Out of Scope

- Zero-input spending capture.
- Household merge flows.
- Historical spending comparison.
- Real-estate affordability.
- Any live account scraping, live rate integration, or financial advice behavior.
